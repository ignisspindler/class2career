Meet Ignis Spindler, PhD

I grew up in a suburban household where nobody had gone to college. No family in business, no family in leadership. I had no model for the right behaviors. No one to show me what successful navigation of a professional environment looked like from the inside.

I got my first PhD and thought it would speak for itself. It didn't. I went into teaching at a public high school, hit every political wall you can hit, and spent five years frustrated and flat. My imposter syndrome got so bad I went back and did a second PhD, this time engineering the outcome deliberately. Student associations. Leadership roles. Befriending career services. Learning how to get hired. Learning how to go straight into management.

After 15 years of working inside what I call the "business translation layer" (communicating expertise in terms of ROI, efficiency, and impact), I am not concerned about employment. I know how to walk into any company and communicate my value from day one.

The price of this toolkit is cheap on purpose. I'm not here to get rich from it. I'm sharing what I wish I could have learned before I made all the mistakes that kept my career flat for so long. You can get a massive head start by starting now.
